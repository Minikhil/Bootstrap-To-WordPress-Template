About Us & History

SBI Legal Assistance offers free legal consultations to all current UB students by a licensed New York State attorney. The attorneys are present at the SBI Legal Assistance office Monday through Friday to offer advice, direction, and assistance regarding any legal matters.

SBI Legal Assistance offers free consultations regarding UB disciplinary policy and procedure through the Student Defender Program. All UB students charged with violations of the UB Student Code of Conduct or Campus Living Community Standards Program are permitted to have an advisor of their choice. A trained roster of UB Law School students, known as Student Defenders, act as student advocates and advisors free of charge.

SBI Legal Assistance offers free notarization services. Each attorney is a Notary Public and can assist UB students with the notarization of documents.

SBI Legal Assistance was developed from a pilot program of a student-run Legal Aid Clinic. This pilot program was a loosely organized legal aid clinic that was funded by the Student Association and was staffed by student volunteers. A practicing attorney visited the office for several hours each week advising students and offering direction and suggestions to the direction of the program.

Notary Public Service

Notary public service is available to UB students. Before an item can be notarized:

  • The individual signing the document must be present.
  • Government issued photo I.D. is required.
  • The document must not be signed prior to notarization.

Student Defender Program

Student Wide Judiciary

All students charged with violations of the UB Student Code of Conduct are permitted to have an advisor of their choice present at all matters heard before the SWJ.

The University Hearing Representative represents the interests of the University, and ought be referred to as the "Campus Prosecutor."

SBI Legal Assistance has a roster of trained U.B. law students, known as Student Defenders, that act as students' advocates and advisors, free of charge.

Campus Living Community Standards Program

All students charged with violations of the Campus Living Community Standards Program are permitted to have a student defender present for the appeals process.
SBI Legal Assistance has a roster of trained U.B. law students, known as Student Defenders, that act as student advocates and advisors, free of charge.

Student Defenders

If you wish to speak with a Student Defender prior to a scheduled hearing, inform the SWJ or Campus Living as soon as possible. The University representative should allow you to call SBI Legal Assistance (716-645-3056) and reschedule your SWJ hearing or Campus Living appeal.

At that point, you should consult a Student Defender as soon as possible. The foremost function of a Student Defender is to inform you of your rights and to provide you with an overview of the SWJ and/or Campus Living appeals process.

Once you talk to a Student Defender, that defender will evaluate your case and offer you advice based upon his or her experience with the SWJ and/or Campus Living. The ultimate decision-making power always remains with you, the student.

If you are dissatisfied with your Student Defender, you may request a consultation with the Chief Defender or Director of SBI Legal Assistance
